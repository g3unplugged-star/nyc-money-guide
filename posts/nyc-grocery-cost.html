How Much Does Grocery Shopping Cost in NYC? (2026 Guide)

Updated: January 2026 · 6 min read

Grocery shopping in New York City can vary drastically depending on where you live, your lifestyle, and how often you cook at home. This guide explains the real cost of groceries in NYC with practical examples and borough-level differences.

Part of our NYC cost of living guide.

Quick Answer:
Most New Yorkers spend between $350 and $700 per month on groceries, depending on household size and shopping habits.

Average Grocery Costs in NYC

Household Type Monthly Cost
Single person $350 – $500
Couple $600 – $800
Family (3–4 people) $900 – $1,200+

Grocery Costs by NYC Borough

Borough Monthly Grocery Cost Notes
Manhattan $450 – $750 Higher prices, more specialty stores
Brooklyn $400 – $650 Wide range by neighborhood
Queens $350 – $600 More affordable ethnic markets
Bronx $300 – $550 Lower average prices

How Grocery Costs Fit Into an NYC Budget

Groceries typically account for 10–15% of a New Yorker’s monthly expenses. Housing remains the largest cost — see how much rent you can afford in NYC.

Ways to Reduce Grocery Costs in NYC

  • Shop at local ethnic markets
  • Use weekly sales and store apps
  • Limit impulse purchases at bodegas
  • Buy store brands when possible
